Top 10 Geometric Tattoo Artists to Follow in 2026

The geometric tattoo world is thriving. From sacred geometry masters to blackwork innovators, these artists are defining the future of geometric body art. Here are the ten accounts you need to follow in 2026.

1. Raul Wesche — @weschetattoo

Based in Houston, Texas, Raul Wesche is arguably the most recognized sacred geometry tattoo artist working today. His large-scale pieces — full sleeves, back panels, and body suits — demonstrate an unmatched understanding of how geometric patterns interact with human anatomy. With nearly 300K followers and a fully booked calendar, Wesche has built Tattoo Temple Houston into a destination for serious collectors.

Style: Sacred geometry, large scale, full body compositions

Why follow: Every post is a masterclass in scale and precision.

2. Chaim Machlev — @dotstolines

Berlin's Chaim Machlev approaches geometric tattooing from a minimalist perspective. Where others go bold, Machlev goes delicate — creating fine-line geometric pieces that feel like whispered mathematics on skin. His work proves that geometric tattoos don't need to be massive to be powerful.

Style: Fine line geometric, minimalist, flow-based

Why follow: The elegance of restraint in geometric design.

3. Okan Uçkun — @oaborundaoiley

Working from Istanbul, Okan draws inspiration from Ottoman geometric traditions — the same patterns found in mosques and palaces — and translates them into bold blackwork tattoos. The result is a fusion of cultural heritage and contemporary tattoo technique that feels both ancient and futuristic.

Style: Blackwork, ornamental geometric, cultural patterns

Why follow: A unique bridge between historical geometry and modern ink.

4. Nikki Sneedle — @nikkisneedle

NYC-based Nikki Sneedle has elevated mandala dotwork into fine art. Each piece is built dot by dot, creating depth and dimension that seems impossible in a two-dimensional medium. Her mandalas are meditative objects — both in creation and in viewing.

Style: Mandala, dotwork, ornamental

Why follow: The patience and precision will leave you speechless.

5. Pokemeon — @pokemeon

Seoul's Pokemeon creates geometric blackwork that looks machine-made — impossibly clean lines, perfect symmetry, and bold compositions that play with negative space. In a city known for fine-line micro tattoos, Pokemeon stands out with work that commands attention from across the room.

Style: Geometric blackwork, abstract, architectural

Why follow: Precision that borders on the supernatural.

6. Kenji Alucky — @black_ink_power

Traveling between Amsterdam and the rest of the world, Kenji Alucky has been a pioneer of geometric blackwork for over a decade. His dotwork and sacred geometry pieces are legendary in the community, and his influence on the current generation of geometric artists is immeasurable.

Style: Dotwork, sacred geometry, blackwork

Why follow: A living legend of the geometric tattoo movement.

7. Ponywave — @ponywave

Toronto-based Ponywave combines geometric patterns with organic elements — flowers growing from sacred geometry, animals composed of mathematical patterns. The hybrid style creates something entirely new: geometric tattoos that feel alive.

Style: Geometric-organic hybrid, illustrative

Why follow: The best of both worlds — geometry meets nature.

8. Maneka — @manekatattoo

Specializing in ornamental and geometric tattoos from her base in Barcelona, Maneka creates intricate pieces that draw from Middle Eastern and South Asian geometric traditions. Her work is detailed, feminine, and architecturally precise.

Style: Ornamental, fine geometric, cultural fusion

Why follow: Geometric elegance with multicultural depth.

9. Thomas Hooper — @thomas__hooper

A Brooklyn institution, Thomas Hooper's sacred geometry work has influenced countless artists. His large-scale pieces — often spanning entire torsos — combine geometric precision with an organic, almost spiritual energy. He's been doing this longer than most, and it shows.

Style: Sacred geometry, large scale, spiritual

Why follow: The OG of American sacred geometry tattooing.

10. Corey Divine — @coreydivine

LA-based Corey Divine brings a fresh perspective to geometric tattooing, incorporating 3D optical illusions and impossible geometries. His work plays with perception — tattoos that appear to float above the skin or tunnel into it.

Style: 3D geometric, optical illusion, sacred geometry

Why follow: Geometry that breaks the fourth wall.

The Geometric Renaissance

What connects all ten of these artists is a shared reverence for the mathematical foundations of their work. They're not just drawing shapes — they're channeling patterns that predate human civilization. As geometric tattooing continues to evolve, these are the accounts leading the charge.
